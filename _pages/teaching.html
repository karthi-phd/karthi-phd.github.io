Teaching and Mentorship

I view education not merely as a transfer of information but as a shared **process of discovery**, where computation and creativity converge to solve complex biological problems. This commitment extends beyond the lecture hall, serving as proof of my strong **communication and mentorship skills**.

Courses and Curriculum Leadership

At SASTRA University, I design and coordinate key specialized courses for undergraduate and postgraduate students in Bioinformatics:

  • **BIN534 – Drug Informatics:** Focuses on computational drug design, structural biology, and cheminformatics workflows.
  • **BIN315 – Immunoinformatics:** Introduces students to systems-level modeling and data analysis in the context of immunology.

My courses are structured around **practical problem-solving**, ensuring students learn not only *how to use* software but *how to think* like scientists—framing questions, choosing appropriate approaches, and interpreting data critically.

Teaching Philosophy: Principles of Applied Learning

My approach emphasizes skills that are immediately transferable to research, organized by three core principles:

Principle Description
Learning Through Doing Modules integrate coding exercises, visualization tasks, and data analysis activities to connect theory to real-world datasets.
Context Before Complexity I emphasize the importance of the biological question first, allowing students to grasp *why* a problem matters before diving into technical mastery.
Integration Modern biology is interdisciplinary. I encourage students to connect molecular modeling, bioinformatics, and systems biology as a unified continuum.

Innovation and Impact

I continuously innovate in the classroom to bridge computational training with tangible research outcomes and promote open science:

  • **Active Learning:** I incorporate **AI-assisted learning**, interactive polls, and case-based discussions.
  • **Project-Based Outcomes:** Students design complex projects (e.g., docking pipelines, cheminformatics workflows) which frequently evolve into successful **research posters or publications**.
  • **Mentorship:** I guide students on computational projects in **drug discovery, antimicrobial resistance, and toxicity prediction**. These projects have consistently resulted in conference presentations and peer-reviewed papers.
  • **Reproducibility:** I promote collaborative learning through **open GitHub repositories** and problem-based workshops, helping students develop crucial reproducible research skills early in their careers.

For me, teaching is an evolving craft where questions are valued over answers, and every student is empowered to contribute meaningfully to science.
