Teaching has been my childhood dream and continues to be the most fulfilling part of my academic journey. Since 2007, I have been teaching undergraduate and postgraduate students at the School of Chemical and Biotechnology, SASTRA Deemed University. Over the years, I have taught a wide range of courses that bridge chemistry, biology, and computation, helping students explore the logic of molecular design and the science behind therapeutic discovery.

My teaching philosophy is centered on the belief that learning should nurture both analytical precision and creative curiosity. I encourage students to connect molecular understanding with biological meaning, and to see the continuity between data, mechanism, and medicine. Every course I teach aims to equip learners with the ability to reason scientifically, interpret molecular data, and apply computational thinking to solve complex biomedical problems.

I integrate interactive, problem-based learning approaches supported by AI and data-centric tools. In recent years, I have introduced modern content such as AI in drug discovery, machine learning in pharmacology, and computational toxicology into existing syllabi. I also use platforms like PollEverywhere and Mentimeter to encourage active participation, while tools such as Schrödinger, PyMOL, and ChatGPT are used for hands-on demonstrations of molecular modeling and scientific reasoning. These innovations have transformed conventional lectures into collaborative sessions where students engage, analyze, and discover.

The courses I teach reflect both depth and diversity across the disciplines of computational and experimental drug discovery:

  • Medicinal Chemistry — explores drug–receptor interactions, structure–activity relationships, and molecular mechanisms of therapeutics.
  • Drug Design — introduces principles of rational drug design, including lead identification, optimization, and target validation.
  • Immunoinformatics — focuses on computational approaches for epitope mapping, vaccine design, and immune response modeling.
  • Applied Bioinformatics — covers biological databases, sequence analysis, and the application of bioinformatics tools in drug discovery.
  • Molecular Modelling and Drug Design — a postgraduate-level course focusing on conformational analysis, molecular docking, and virtual screening.
  • Drug Informatics — integrates cheminformatics, pharmacokinetic modeling, and machine learning approaches in drug development.

Mentorship is an integral part of my teaching identity. I have supervised several B.Tech and M.Tech research projects that bridge computation and biology. Many of my students have gone on to present their work at conferences, publish in peer-reviewed journals, and secure positions in academia and industry. I view each project as an opportunity to cultivate scientific curiosity, independence, and integrity in young researchers.

Teaching, to me, is more than delivering knowledge; it is about inspiring inquiry and building confidence in scientific reasoning. I believe that the best classrooms are those where students learn to think critically, collaborate effectively, and find joy in discovery. Through continuous innovation and mentorship, I aim to create a learning environment that connects molecular insight with translational impact, preparing students for a future shaped by data, discovery, and interdisciplinary science.
