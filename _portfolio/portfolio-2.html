My research often extends beyond academic publication into practical applications. The following patents reflect collaborative work aimed at developing new therapeutic compounds for infectious diseases through a combination of computational modeling, medicinal chemistry, and biological validation.

International Patent (PCT)

WO2023067625, Antifungal Compound, Composition and Uses Thereof
PCT/IN2022/050932, filed on 19 October 2022 and published on 27 April 2023.
Applicants: SASTRA Deemed University, Versitech Limited
Inventors: Neelakantan P, Solomon A P, Shanmugam K
National phase: India (2024), China (2024), United States (2025)

This invention describes a novel antifungal compound and formulation with broad activity against human pathogenic fungi. The compound was identified through structure-based design and validated for its ability to inhibit fungal growth and biofilm formation. The work represents a collaborative effort combining computational screening and in vitro evaluation to identify next-generation antifungal candidates.

Indian Patent

IN202241054663, An Anti-infective Agent, Method of Treatment, and Use Thereof
Filed on 23 September 2022 and published on 15 September 2023.
Applicant: SASTRA Deemed University
Inventors: Shanmugam K, Solomon A P, Neelakantan P
Status: Awaiting examination

This patent covers an anti-infective compound with antibacterial and antibiofilm properties. The molecule was developed through computational docking and pharmacophore modeling and later validated using in vitro infection models. The invention offers a new approach for controlling biofilm-associated infections and antibiotic resistance.

Focus Areas

Drug discovery, Anti-infective compounds, Biofilm inhibition, Translational bioinformatics, Natural product chemistry
